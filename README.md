# Merritt Coil Bird Exposure Cage

This repository contains technical drawing sheets and cutting files for a bird exposure cage designed for use inside a Merritt coil system.

The cage is intended for temporary exposure of birds in magnetic-field experiments. The design uses non-magnetic materials and is suitable for placement within the homogeneous field zone of the coil system.

## Repository contents

The repository includes two main types of files:

1. **Drawing sheets**
   Technical drawing sheets with the dimensions required for manufacturing and assembly.

2. **Cutting files**
   DXF files prepared for CNC cutting, laser cutting, milling, or routing of cage parts.

## Recommended material

We recommend using **9 mm plywood** for manufacturing the cage parts.

Before cutting, check that the material thickness specified in the DXF/CAM setup matches the actual thickness of the plywood.

## Cage top

The top of the cage should be covered with a **soft fishing net**.

The net should be fixed securely to prevent birds from escaping, while still allowing safe access and ventilation.

## Handling sleeves

The openings used for catching and removing birds from the cage should be fitted with **sleeves made of soft fishing net**.

The sleeves should be woven or sewn into the access openings. They allow the experimenter to reach inside the cage and safely catch or remove a bird while reducing the risk of escape.

## Notes before cutting

Before manufacturing, please check:

* material thickness;
* DXF units and scale;
* tool diameter or laser kerf;
* kerf compensation;
* inner and outer contours;
* hole diameters;
* slot dimensions;
* fit between parts;
* whether the design matches the required dimensions of the Merritt coil system.

## Assembly notes

During assembly, avoid ferromagnetic components. If screws, hinges, or other fasteners are required, use non-magnetic materials whenever possible.

After assembly, the cage should be checked for:

* mechanical stability;
* absence of sharp edges;
* secure fixation of the net;
* safe access through the handling sleeves;
* compatibility with the available space inside the Merritt coil system.
